\subsection{PSD models}
\label{sec:PSD_models}
\note{Various parts here may/will be moved to the methods section. Should also include physical motivation/interpretations for noise properties, e.g. reasons behind values of $\alpha$ and $\fknee$.}

We follow the \note{standard} convention, and assume that a $1/f$-model can be used to fit the noise PSD
\begin{equation}
    \label{eq:psd_oof_model}
    P(f) = \sigma_0^2 \bclosed{1 + \pclosed{\frac{f}{\fknee}}^\alpha},
\end{equation}
where $f$ denotes temporal frequency, $\sigma_0$ denotes white-noise level, $\alpha$ is the slope of the correlated noise spectrum, and $\fknee$ roughly marks the frequency of the transition between white noise and correlated noise.  

While this model works \pnote{has worked} well for many instruments \pnote{citations}, we find that the noise properties in AKARI deviate from this standard model with a prominent increase/rise in the noise levels towards higher frequencies. This feature is evident across all \pnote{?} detectors in every band. To account for this, we extend the model by introducing a second $1/f$-term, and adopt the following model \pnote{I'm tempted to use different letters/variables for the second $1/f$-term to avoid the subscripts.}
\begin{equation}
    \label{eq:psd_2oof_model}
    P(f) = \sigma_0^2 \bclosed{1 + \pclosed{\frac{f}{\fkneei[1]}}^{\slopei[1]} + \pclosed{\frac{f}{\fkneei[2]}}^{\slopei[2]}}.
\end{equation}
Here, subscript $1$ refers to the standard parametrization of the $1/f$ model, whereas $2$ refers to the noise increase towards high frequencies. This particular model was chosen to properly fit the noise data at high frequencies in a minimal way \pnote{bad sentence}. 
The main restrictions/trait of our model parameters is that $\slopei[1]<0$, whereas $\slopei[2]>0$. Additionally, we require $\fkneei[1]<\fkneei[2]<f_{\mathrm{samp}}/2$ \pnote{elaborate}.

The five PSD parameters we will sample are thus $\xi^n = \{\sigma_0,\alpha_1,\fkneei[1],\alpha_2,\fkneei[2]\}$.
