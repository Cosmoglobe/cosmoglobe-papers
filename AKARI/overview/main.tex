   \title{\bfseries{\Cosmoglobe\ DR3. I. Global Bayesian end-to-end analysis of AKARI-FIS.}}

   \input{authors.tex}

   \titlerunning{\Cosmoglobe: AKARI reanalysis}
   \authorrunning{E.~Gjerl{\o}w et al.}

   \date{\today} 
   
   \abstract{We present a Bayesian end-to-end reanalysis of the AKARI Far-Infrared Surveyor (FIS) data within the Cosmoglobe analysis framework. Between 2006 and 2007, AKARI-FIS observed more than 96\% of the sky in four wavelength bands: N60, WIDE-S, WIDE-L, and N160, corresponding to central wavelengths of 65, 90, 140 and 160 microns, respectively. The analysis is conducted jointly between the AKARI time-ordered (TOD) data, \Planck 545 GHz and 857 GHz map-level data, TOD-level from the DIRBE bands between 240 and 12 microns, and FIRAS data. Our sky model includes a four-component dust model, a cosmic infrared background (CIB) component, a novel zodiacal light model, and a \CII model. This joint setup of sky model and multiple experiments allows for an estimation of AKARI gains and systematic effects that are consistent with the other observations involved, and allows for a final sky model that takes into account all relevant data.
       We describe the AKARI instrumental noise as white noise plus a correlated noise term, which is modelled with a two-component $1/f$ power spectral density (PSD). Glitches are modelled using a super-glitch model, which handles the glitches so well, and we model crosstalk using the crosstalk modelling method. The handling of other systematic errors, such as ramp glitch corrections, ramp gain nonlinearities and detector pixel pointing offsets is also presented.
       The result of this analysis is i) A high-resolution four-component dust model that is nevertheless characterized by far fewer parameters than other dust models ii) High-resolution maps of the CIB fluctuations, iii) A high-resolution zodiacal light model that is characterized as a combination of blackbodies, and iv) High-resolution \CII maps. We present comparisons to the original AKARI data analysis, and we find that the joint reanalysis, benefitting from algorithmic developments over the last two decades, as well as being informed by other experiment and a robust sky model, is able to improve constraints on all the abovementioned science targets.
   }
