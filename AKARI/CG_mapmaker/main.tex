   \date{\today} 
   
   \abstract{Cross-talk between detectors, both of optical and electrical nature, is one of the long-standing issues of Akari FIS data, at the core of \textsc{Cosmoglobe} DR3. 
   We explore the implementation of a conjugate gradient mapmaking method and demonstrate its ability of accounting for the detectors cross talk through simulated point source maps.
   We compare the method's performance, and its effects on the noise properties of the solution maps, with a traditional inverse-variance bin mapmaker.

\section{Methods}
[Motivation for having a CG-driven mapmaker to solve the problem.]

In a setup where the instrument presents significant cross-talk between detectors, the data can be modeled in time domain as
\begin{equation}
	\textbf{d} = \mathrm{W}\textbf{s} + \textbf{n}
\end{equation}
where $\textbf{s}$ is the true sky signal projected from the sky map $\textbf{m}_s$ through the pointing matrix P as $\textbf{s} =
\mathrm{P}\, \textbf{m}_s$; 
P has size of ($n_{pix}$,~$n_{tod}$) as it maps between pixel space and time ordered data.
