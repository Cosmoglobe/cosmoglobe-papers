\section{Introduction}
~\cite{CG02_05} first introduced the high spatial correlation between \CII\ and hot thermal dust emission, along with pointing out that the high frequency DIRBE data can be almost entirely explained by fitting five templates, which have a physically motivated correlation to thermal dust: the aforementioned \CII, as seen by FIRAS \citep{fixsen:1998}; the HI4PI \HI\ line emission map~\citep{HI4PI:2016}; the \Gaia\ dust extinction map \citep{edenhofer:2024}; the WHAM H$\alpha$ line emission map \citep{wham:2003,2016WHAM}; and, lastly, the Dame CO line emission map \citep{dame2001}. Building upon this work,~\cite{CG02_06} defined a three component dust model based on four high resolution templates. Two of those templates are the same as initially used by~\cite{CG02_05}: the \gaia\ and the WHAM H$\alpha$ templates, where the first is identified as a nearby dust component. The other two are defined as cold and hot dust, and a template for each one is built off of the high resolution \hfi\ data, assuming that at the \hfi\ frequency range, each of these dust populations behave as a modified black body, and subtracting the WHAM H$\alpha$ template as a correction to the hot dust component.~\cite{CG02_07} then takes these four high resolution templates and applies them to the high frequency DIRBE data. 

In this paper, we follow from that work and apply this three component dust model, intially, to the \Planck\ High-Frequnecy Instrument (HFI), and the \COBE- DIRBE and FIRAS data, making sure to exclude any channels that would be contaminated with \CII\ line emission, in order to build an absolutely calibrated prior spectral energy density (SED) for the AKARI reanalysis. We then use the high-$\ell$ power from the AKARI FIS data in order to further refine the relative amplitudes of the three components. The full SED is then built as: a modified black body, from the best-fit analysis to the \Planck\ \hfi\ data, until 857\,GHz; a spline based on \aism{n} points, fit to the absolutely calibrated FIRAS and DIRBE data, between 857\,GHz and \aism{search when astrodust starts}; and an amplitude fit of the astrodust \aism{cite} SED model to the DIRBE data.
