   \title{\bfseries{\Cosmoglobe\ DR3. Q. High-resolution map of Galactic 158\micron{} \CII{} emission in AKARI data}}

   \input{authors.tex}

   \titlerunning{\Cosmoglobe: AKARI CII map}
   \authorrunning{N.-O.~Stutzer et al.}

   \date{\today} 
   
   \abstract{
    We present a new high-resolution full-sky map of the Galactic \CII{} emission using the AKARI WIDE-L and N60 data within the \cosmoglobe{} global Bayesian analysis framework. In addition to the \CII{} line at 158\micron{}, thermal dust strongly contributes to the bandpass-integrated signal measured by the AKARI WIDE-L and N160 bands at $120$--$200\micron$. As the bandpasses of detectors within each band can vary by several 10\% at any given frequency, while their integrals remain stable, we can extract the \CII emission from the bandpass integrated thermal dust by differencing detector maps (Type A). Alternatively, we incorporate the \CII{} line into the global \cosmoglobe sky model and extract the component as part of the joint fit using both AKARI FIS, Planck HFI and \COBE{} FIRAS data (Type B). Both Type A and B maps are consistent with each other, although the latter provides a superior signal to noise ratio. The resulting maps are the highest resolution \CII{} estimate across the full sky to date, with noise level estimates ranging from $XXX$–$YYYY$\MJysr and a resolution of $\sim37''$ FWHM. Cross-correlating our \CII{} map to that of FIRAS, as well as to the \cosmoglobe{} DR3 hot dust map, we respectively find correlation coefficients of $0.XX$ and $0.YY$, showing a high degree of spacial correlation to both. As \CII{} is one of the most important cooling mechanisms in the insterstellar medium (ISM) we can estimate the star-formation rate (SFR) in each pixel of the sky by using the projected surface brightness of \CII{} from our maps. The resulting SFR map is broadly consistant with [OTHER SURVEY(S)] in a selection of [SOME WELL-KNOWN STAR-FORMING REGIONS] and represents the currently highest resolution full-sky SFR map of our own Galaxy in the literature.
