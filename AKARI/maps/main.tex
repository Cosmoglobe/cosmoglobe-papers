   \title{\bfseries{\Cosmoglobe\ DR3. Q. Improved high-resolution AKARI FIS frequency maps with end-to-end error propagation}}

   \input{authors.tex}

   \titlerunning{\Cosmoglobe: AKARI FIS reanalysis}
   \authorrunning{K.~A.~Glasscock et al.}

   \date{\today} 
   
   \abstract{
	   We present new AKARI Far-Infrared Surveyor (FIS) frequency sky maps derived through global Bayesian end-to-end analysis of AKARI, Planck and COBE-DIRBE. 
%
Our analysis jointly samples astrophysical, instrumental, and calibration parameters while iteratively reconstructing the sky signal from the time-ordered data. This approach enables consistent propagation of uncertainties and accounts for correlations between sky components and instrumental effects. We generate posterior samples of all model parameters and derive posterior mean frequency maps and uncertainty products for each AKARI-FIS band.
%
   The resulting posterior mean maps are compared to the legacy AKARI \comment{and Planck?} frequency maps and show good agreement. The most important quality improvement is the preservation of the full angular resolution ($\sim$ 0.5-1\si{\arcminute}) of the AKARI-FIS observations and all frequency maps are provided at $\texttt{NSIDE}=16000$. We further characterise map uncertainties, convergence properties, and residual artefacts through posterior covariance estimates and goodness-of-fit analyses.
%
   Notably, we achieve \comment{a white noise RMS level of \SI{0.06}{\mega\jansky\per\steradian}}. %whatever lets us see CIB, for 140um we need it down below 0.7 MJys/sr per pixel I think.
These products provide the highest-fidelity all-sky AKARI-FIS maps to date and establish a statistically rigorous foundation for future far-infrared analyses. 
   }
